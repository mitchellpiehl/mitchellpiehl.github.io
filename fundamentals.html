The Transformer

The foundation for modern NLP models. Uses self-attention and cross-attention mechanisms to process input sequences in parallel, making them significantly more efficient than RNN-based models. Introduced in 2017 in the paper titled “Attention Is All You Need”

The Convolutional Neural Network (CNN)

A specialized DNN designed for processing grid-like data such as images. CNNs use convolutional layers to help detect patterns such as edges, textures, and shapes by applying kernels across an image. Frequently, many convolutional layers are used to abstract more complex patterns. CNNs are commonly used in image classification, and tasks such as facial recognition.

Artificial Neural Network

A computational model inspired by the structure and function of the human brain. Consists of layers of neurons, including an input layer, one or more hidden layers, and an output layer. Every connection has an associated weight and every neuron has an applied activation function such as sigmoid or ReLU. ANNs learn by adjusting weights of connections through optimization techniques such as backpropagation and gradient descent.
