Further readings are in order from most to least recommended

**More technical based further readings are noted with asteriks**

Artificial Intelligence - A Guide for Thinking Humans

Author: Melanie Mitchell

Published: 2019

A great introduction to AI that discusses the history of artificial intelligence and major accomplishments. Includes details of major architectures within AI, and limitations of these systems. Talks about the future of AI and fears that come along with it.

A Brief History of Artificial Intelligence

Author: Michael Wooldridge

Published: 2021

An in depth explanation of the history of AI presenting in a mostly chronological order.

The Information: A History, a Theory, a Flood

Author: James Gleick

Published: 2011

Provides the history of information, including topics of Charles Babbage and Ada Lovelace, Claude Shannon and Information Theory, and the ideas of machines thinking.

**The Deep Learning Revolution**

Author: Terrence J. Sejnowski

Published: 2018

A more technical exploration into the history of AI that explains the major breakthroughs of deep learning and the work of the author Terrence Sejnowski.

**The Cambridge Handbook of Artificial Intelligence**

Edited by Keith Frankish and William M. Ramsey

Published: 2014

In depth handbook...

The Worlds I See

Author: Fei Fei Li

Published: 2023

Autobiography of the life of Dr. Fei-Fei Li, the founding director of the Stanford Institute for Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence. Goes into fine detail of how ImageNet was created, as well as the motivation behind Human-Centered AI, and AI4All. Talks about life growing up in China then moving to America as well as the big AI developments that happen during that time.

Stanford Encylopedia of Philosophy

Published: 2018

Introduction to the history of AI, what AI is, different types of AI, and the philosophy of AI.
