Alan Turing

1912-1954

A British mathematician and logician who introduced the concept of the Turing Machine and proposed the Turing Test as a criteria for machine intelligence. His ideas remain central to the philosophical foundations of the field.

George Boole

1815-1864

19th-century mathematician and logician who introduced Boolean algebra in 1854 in his work titled The Laws of Thought, the system of logic that is the foundation of digital computing. His work helped create programming languages, formal sytems, and modern circuits.

Image Source: Wikipedia

John Searle

1932-

A distinguished American philosopher and proponent of materialism who is best known for his critique of AI through the now famous Chinese Room thought experiment. The thought experiment argues that computers do not possess a genuine understanding of content. His critiques may be controversial, but they force researchers and philosophers to think more deeply about the meaning of consciousness.

Aristotle

384-322 BCE

Ancient Greek philosopher whose work laid the foundations for logic and metaphysics. His formal logic system and syllogism created a structured form of deductive reasoning. His formal logic was also the underlying system behind symbolic AI.

Image Source: Wikipedia

David Chalmers

1966-

A contemporary philosopher who introduced the "hard problem of consciousness" in his 1995 work titled The Conscious Mind. He distinguishes the easy problem as explaining behaviors, and the hard problem as how physical processes in the brain give rise to subjective experience.

Rene Descartes

1596-1650

A French philosopher and mathematician who introduced dualism. His famous statement "I think, therefore I am" emphasizes his belief that humans are thinking things, and the only things capable of thinking. His seperation of mind and body in dualism helped influence debates of machine learning.

Euclid

300 BCE

Ancient Greek mathematician known as the "father of geometry" due to his work titled Elements. Elements applied a axiomatic approach that is known the standard for formal systems in math, logic, and AI. He was able to form complex truths from foundational assumptions, similar to how AI systems use rules to make logical inferences.

Bertrand Russell

1872-1970

A British philosopher, logician, and mathematician who co-authored Principia Mathematica, which aimed to derive all of mathematics from logical axioms. He is also know for Russell's Paradox which exposed inconsistencies in naive set theory. This exposure then led to more advanced set theory.

  • View Person

Image Source: Wikipedia

Gottlob Frege

1848-1925

A German mathematician and philosopher who founded modern logic by introducing a formal language in his 1879 work titled Begriffssschrift that could express mathematical and logical relationships better than Aristotelian logic. His predicate calculus was very influential to math and computer science by introducing variables, quantifers, and functions.

Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz

1646-1716

A German polymath who envisioned a universal logical language a machine that could reason. He also invented one of the first mechanical calculators. His ideas that the world could be reduced to calculation are now central to AI.

The Stoics

Around 300 BCE

A school of ancient Greek philosophy that developed an early form of propositional logic, which included truth tables and logical connectives such as if-then, or either-or.
