John McCarthy

1927-2011

John McCarthy was a computer scientist and cognitive scientist known as the father of AI. He coined the term "Artificial Intelligence" during the 1956 Dartmouth Conference that has assembled, and developed LISP, a key programming language for AI research. His work has laid the groundwork for logical reasoning and machine learning.

Marvin Minsky

1927-2016

As a cognitive scientist and co-founder of the MIT AI lab, he was a key figure in the early development of AI. He contributed both to the theoretical and philosophical foundations of AI by simulating machines that could simulate human reasoning. His approach combined computer science with psychology and neuroscience, shaping ideas of machine consciousness.

Geoffrey Hinton

1946-

A crucial figure in the development of neural networks and deep learning. Has multiple massive contributions to the field of deep learning including co-developing the back propagation algorithm, the foundation for modern neural nets, and winning the 2012 ImageNet competition with a deep convolutional neural network, showing the potential of CNN's in image classification.

Yann LeCun

1960-

One of the most influential figures in the field of computer vision for developing the convolutional neural network (CNN) and LeNet, a outstanding handwritten digit classifier. He is currently the Chief AI Scientist at Meta and a professor at NYU.

Allen Newell

1927-1992

One of the co-founders of AI who attended the Dartmouth Conference. He developed some of the earliest AI programs including the Logic Theorist and General Problem Solver (GPS). His work was the basis for symbolic AI. His work also showed how human-like problem solving could be modeled computationally.

Herbert Simon

1916-2001

A early pioneer of AI, who attended the Dartmouth Conference and developed some of the first AI programs. A strong believer that intelligence could be understood scientifically and therefore replicated in machines. He also helped develop the Logic Theorist and General Problem Solver (GPS) with Allen Newell.

Andrew Ng

1976-

A leading figure in modern AI. He co-founded Google Brain and founded Coursera, the online learning platform. His courses on AI and machine learning have so far reached millions of people.
